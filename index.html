Ultimate Research Goal

Discover & Engineer Wormholes

A wormhole — the Einstein–Rosen bridge — is a theoretical tunnel through the spacetime fabric connecting two distant points in the universe, or even two separate universes. Predicted by general relativity, a traversable wormhole would require exotic matter with negative energy density to hold it open. I deeply study Kip Thorne’s traversable wormhole models, the energy conditions that permit or forbid them, and how quantum field theory near a wormhole throat might stabilise the structure. This physics is the foundation of true interstellar travel for humanity.

Deep Study #1

How Spacetime Works

Spacetime is the four-dimensional fabric of reality — three dimensions of space fused inseparably with time, as Einstein revealed in Special Relativity (1905) and General Relativity (1915). Mass and energy don’t simply exist inside spacetime — they warp and curve it. That curvature is gravity itself. Time is not absolute: a clock on Earth ticks measurably slower than one orbiting in weak gravity (gravitational time dilation, confirmed by GPS satellites every day). I study spacetime geodesics — the curved paths objects follow through warped spacetime — the light cone structure that defines causality, and the extreme spacetime topology near singularities where wormhole geometry becomes possible.

4D Continuum Time Dilation Geodesics Lorentz Transform Light Cones

Deep Study #2

How Gravity Really Works

Gravity is not a force being transmitted through space — it is the shape of spacetime itself. Massive objects create curvature, and other objects simply follow the straightest possible path (geodesic) through that curved geometry, which looks like attraction. I deeply explore gravitational waves — ripples in spacetime fabric detected by LIGO in 2015, confirming Einstein’s 100-year-old prediction — the Kerr metric describing rotating black holes and their frame-dragging effect, and the deepest open problem in physics: reconciling gravity with quantum mechanics. Theories like loop quantum gravity and string theory attempt this unification, and the answer likely holds the key to stable wormholes.

General Relativity Gravitational Waves Kerr Metric Loop Quantum Gravity LIGO

Deep Study #3

Is the Multiverse Real?

The multiverse is not science fiction — it emerges independently from several serious physics frameworks. Eternal Inflation theory predicts our Big Bang was one bubble nucleating in an endless inflationary sea, producing countless causally disconnected universes. Hugh Everett’s Many-Worlds Interpretation of quantum mechanics says every quantum event branches reality into parallel histories — every outcome happens, in a different branch. String theory’s landscape predicts ~10500 possible universes each with different physical constants. I study CMB anomalies that could be signatures of bubble universe collisions, the fine-tuning problem (why are physical constants so perfectly set for life?), and whether wormholes could theoretically serve as bridges between branches of the multiverse.

Many-Worlds (Everett) Eternal Inflation String Landscape CMB Anomalies Fine-Tuning Problem

Deep Study #4

How Dimensions Work

We experience three spatial dimensions, but theoretical physics is certain more exist. In 1921, Kaluza proposed a 5th dimension to unify gravity and electromagnetism — it worked mathematically. Modern String Theory demands 10 dimensions; M-Theory requires 11. The extra dimensions are compactified at the Planck length (~10⁻³⁵ m), curled so tightly they are undetectable with current instruments. These hidden dimensions aren’t empty — they determine the physical constants of our universe. I study the Randall–Sundrum braneworld model, where our universe is a 4D membrane floating in a higher-dimensional bulk, and how extra dimensions could stabilise traversable wormhole geometry — directly connecting dimensional physics to my wormhole engineering goal.
